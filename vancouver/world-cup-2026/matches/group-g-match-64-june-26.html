BC Place Stadium Vancouver

BC Place Stadium hosts this David vs Goliath Group G clash between underdog New Zealand and European powerhouse Belgium. The All Whites will look to cause one of the biggest upsets in World Cup history.

Stadium Facts

  • Seating Capacity: 54,500 for FIFA World Cup matches
  • Roof Type: Retractable roof (covered stadium)
  • Surface: Artificial turf (temporary natural grass for World Cup)
  • Opened: 1983
  • Location: Downtown Vancouver, British Columbia

Frequently Asked Questions

What time does New Zealand vs Belgium start in Vancouver?

The match kicks off at 8:00 PM Pacific Time on Friday, June 26, 2026 at BC Place Stadium.

Who is Belgium's best player?

Kevin De Bruyne of Manchester City is widely considered Belgium's best player and one of the greatest midfielders in the world. His playmaking ability is exceptional.

Can New Zealand beat Belgium?

Upsets happen at every World Cup! New Zealand went undefeated at the 2010 World Cup, including a draw against Italy. While Belgium are heavy favorites, anything is possible.

Is this a good match for neutral fans to attend?

Absolutely! You get to see world-class players like Kevin De Bruyne, and there's always the chance of witnessing a historic upset. It's a great Friday night football experience.

How early should I arrive for the match?

We recommend arriving at least 2 hours before kick-off. For an 8:00 PM game, that means arriving around 6:00 PM. This gives you time for security, finding your seat, and soaking in the atmosphere.

Is BC Place easy to get to on a Friday evening?

Yes, BC Place is well-served by public transit. The SkyTrain runs frequently, even on Friday evenings. Just be aware that there may be more people heading to the stadium as it's a weekend game.
