Pete Fry Announces Run for Vancouver Mayor in 2026 Election

Published: January 30, 2026

VANCOUVER, BC ‚Äì Longtime City Councillor Pete Fry has officially entered the race to become the next Mayor of Vancouver. Representing the Vancouver Green Party, Fry announced his candidacy on Friday, January 30, 2026, promising a fresh approach to leadership at City Hall.

A Focus on Empathy and Collaboration

Pete Fry, a two-term councillor first elected in 2018, says he is running to bring "empathy and collaboration" back to the city. He criticized the current leadership under Mayor Ken Sim, stating that Vancouver should not be "run like a business." Instead, Fry wants a government that listens to its residents and works across party lines to solve big issues.

Key Issues for the 2026 Campaign

Fry‚Äôs platform focuses on making Vancouver a more livable and fair city. His top priorities include:

Affordable Housing: Focusing on "missing middle" housing, such as six-storey buildings that can be built quickly and affordably.

Community Safety: Shifting the focus toward mental health support and well-being rather than just traditional policing.

Better Governance: Improving transparency and restoring the power of the Vancouver Park Board and the Integrity Commissioner.

Renter Protections: Strengthening rights for the many people who rent in Vancouver.

Who is Pete Fry?

Pete Fry has lived in Vancouver since he was a child. Before entering politics, he was a graphic designer and a community advocate in the Strathcona neighborhood. He is also the son of longtime Vancouver Centre MP Hedy Fry.

Why This Matters for Vancouver

This is a historic moment for the Vancouver Green Party, as it is the first time they have ever run a candidate for mayor. Fry joins a crowded field for the October 2026 municipal election, aiming to unite progressive voters who want a change from the current administration.
