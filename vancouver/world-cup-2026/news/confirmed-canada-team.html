Canada FIFA World Cup 2026 Roster Announcement Tonight

Canada's 26-man squad for the FIFA World Cup 2026 will be revealed tonight, Friday, May 29, 2026. For fans in Vancouver, the announcement starts at 4:00 PM Pacific Time, which is 7:00 PM Eastern Time.

Where to Watch the Canada Roster Announcement

Canada Soccer says the roster reveal will air live on TSN, CTV, Crave, and RDS. Streaming viewers can watch through Crave's live CTV channel, where available.

What to Watch For

Jesse Marsch has been working from a 32-player training camp group in Charlotte, so six players must miss out when the final 26-man team is named. The biggest questions are likely around final defenders, midfield depth, and player fitness.

Alphonso Davies, Jonathan David, Stephen Eustaquio, Tajon Buchanan, Cyle Larin, Alistair Johnston, Maxime Crepeau, and Dayne St. Clair are among the major names fans will be watching closely. For British Columbia fans, there is also local interest in players with Vancouver or B.C. links, including Ali Ahmed, Ralph Priso, Niko Sigur, and Joel Waterman.

Why It Matters for Vancouver

Canada will play two group-stage matches in Vancouver at BC Place: Canada vs Qatar on June 18 and Switzerland vs Canada on June 24. Tonight's roster announcement tells local fans which players could step onto the field in Vancouver during the country's biggest soccer moment.

After the roster reveal, Canada plays send-off matches against Uzbekistan on June 1 in Edmonton and the Republic of Ireland on June 5 in Montreal before opening the World Cup on June 12 in Toronto.

Sources: Canada Soccer roster reveal announcement and Canada Soccer Charlotte training camp squad.
